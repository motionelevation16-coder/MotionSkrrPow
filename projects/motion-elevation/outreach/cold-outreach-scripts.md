# Motion Elevation — Cold Outreach Scripts
### LinkedIn DMs + Instagram DMs + Cold Email Templates

**Target:** Solopreneurs, SMBs, local businesses  
**Tone:** Direct, conversational, value-first — NOT salesy  
**Rule:** Never pitch in the first message. Open conversations, not close deals.

---

## PART 1: LinkedIn DM Scripts (3 Variations)

### LinkedIn Rules First
- Connect request note: Keep under 300 chars, no pitch
- First message after connect: Observe something specific about THEM
- Pitch only after they respond and show interest
- Best day/time: Tuesday–Thursday, 8–10am or 3–5pm local time

---

### LinkedIn Variation A — "Specific Observation" (Best for coaches, consultants)

**Connection Request Note:**
> Hey [Name], came across your content on [topic they posted about] — great perspective. Would love to connect.

**First Message (after accepting):**
> Hey [Name], noticed you're working with [type of clients] — congrats on the traction.
>
> Quick question: do you have an automated system for following up with leads who don't book, or is that still mostly manual for you?
>
> Asking because I've been helping coaches in your space fix exactly that — curious what your setup looks like.

**If they reply "it's manual" or "not really":**
> That's actually super common. Most coaches I talk to are losing 40–60% of potential clients just from slow follow-up.
>
> We build that entire system in 48 hours — follows up via email + SMS within minutes, books the call, sends reminders. All automatic.
>
> Would it make sense to have a quick 15-minute chat to see if it'd be a fit for your setup? No pressure — just want to show you what it looks like.

**Signal to watch:** If they ask "how does it work?" or "what do you charge?" — that's buying intent. Move to call booking immediately.

---

>
> Would a 15-min call make sense to see if it'd work for your setup?

## PART 2: Instagram DM Scripts (3 Variations)

### Instagram Rules First
- Best for: coaches, course creators, e-commerce brands, local businesses with active IG
- Start with a genuine comment on a recent post BEFORE DMing
- DMs work best when they've already seen you engaging with their content
- Keep first DM short — under 4 lines

---

### Instagram Variation A — "Post Reaction" (After commenting on their post)

**First DM (after commenting on post):**
> Hey [Name] — just commented on your post about [topic]. That really resonated.
>
> Quick one: do you currently have automation set up for when people DM you or reach out through your link in bio? Or is it still manual replies?

**If they say manual:**
> That's the most common thing I hear from coaches/creators your size.
>
> We set up AI-powered response systems that reply instantly, qualify leads, and book calls — even at 2am. Clients see 2–3x more booked calls within the first week.
>
> Want me to send you a quick breakdown of how it works?

**If they say yes:**
> Awesome. Here's a quick 60-second overview: [Loom video link or brief written breakdown]
>
> If it makes sense for you, I can show you a live example on a quick call. No pitch — just showing you the thing.

---

### Instagram Variation B — "Reels/Stories Response" (After watching their story or reel)

**First DM:**
> Hey! Just watched your reel on [topic] — loved it.
>
> Random question: how are you currently handling the DMs + inquiries that come in after your content blows up? Manual, or do you have something set up?

**If manual:**
> Yeah, most people at your stage are managing that manually — which works until it doesn't.
>
> I help people like you set up systems that handle the follow-up automatically, so you can post and step away without losing leads.
>
> Want to see how that looks? Takes about 10 mins to walk through.

---

### Instagram Variation C — "Problem-First" (Cold, no prior engagement)

**First DM (use sparingly — lower conversion rate):**
> Hey [Name]! Love what you're building.
>
> I help [coaches/store owners/their niche] automate the parts of their business that eat up time — lead follow-up, onboarding, payments. Usually takes 48h to set up and pays for itself in the first month.
>
> Would it be worth a quick chat to see if it fits your setup?

**Follow-up if no reply (3 days later):**
> Hey, no worries if now's not a good time.
>
> Just wanted to leave this here: the #1 reason businesses our size plateau is not lack of leads — it's lack of systems to convert them.
>
> Open to a free 15-min audit anytime. Zero pressure.

## PART 3: Cold Email Templates (3 Variations)

### Cold Email Rules First
- Subject line = highest leverage. Test relentlessly.
- Keep total email under 150 words for first outreach
- One clear CTA only
- Personalize the first line — generic = trash
- Best send time: Tuesday–Thursday, 7–9am recipient's timezone

---

### Cold Email Variation A — "Quick Question" (Highest reply rate format)

**Subject:** quick question about [Company Name]'s follow-up process

**Body:**
> Hey [First Name],
>
> Found [Company Name] while looking at [niche] businesses in [city/region].
>
> One thing I noticed: most [their niche] businesses are leaving money on the table with slow lead follow-up. When someone fills out a form or sends an inquiry, the first 5 minutes are critical — and most businesses respond hours later (or not at all).
>
> We fix that. Automated follow-up that responds in under 60 seconds, qualifies the lead, and books the call. Built in 48 hours.
>
> Is this something you're actively thinking about, or is your current process already solid?
>
> [Your Name]  
> Motion Elevation  
> [Phone / Calendly link]

**Subject line alternatives to test:**
- "your lead follow-up — quick thought"
- "question for [Company Name]"
- "noticed something about your [clinic/shop/agency]"

---

## Quick Personalization Checklist (For Each Outreach)

Before sending, verify:
- [ ] First line references something SPECIFIC about them (post, ad, product, location)
- [ ] Pain point matches their obvious business type
- [ ] You've used their actual first name
- [ ] CTA is one specific action (not "let me know if interested")
- [ ] No typos (run through Grammarly)
- [ ] Subject line doesn't sound automated
