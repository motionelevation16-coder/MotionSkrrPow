## Mobile Games Market Overview

### Key Numbers (2024-2025)
- **ARPU (Average Revenue Per User):** ~$36
- **Total market:** Games = majority of mobile consumer spending
- **Top game (Honor of Kings):** $2.5B in 2025
- **Royal Match:** $1.46B in 2024 (up 50% YoY) — casual puzzle game

### Hyper-Casual Economics
| Metric | Android | iOS |
|--------|---------|-----|
| CPI (Cost Per Install) | $1.50 | $2.50 |
| LTV (Lifetime Value) | ~$2.80 | ~$2.80 |

**Reality:** Thin margins. Need VOLUME. CPI must stay below LTV or you bleed money.

### Hybrid-Casual (The Sweet Spot)
- Hyper-casual mechanics + meta features + better monetization
- Genre nearly **doubled revenue** in 2024
- Hit **$219M** in H1 2024
- Top 100 hyper-casual: **2.4B downloads** in H1 2025
- IAP revenue from hybrid-casual: **$345M**

### Idle Arcade Games
- Top 50 idle arcade games: **$6.3M** in October 2024 alone
- Growing genre, trending hybrid-casual
- Retention: D1 25%, D7 3-7% (better than hyper-casual)

### Real Revenue Examples
| Game | Weekly Revenue | Notes |
|------|----------------|-------|
| Physics puzzler (indie) | $31K → $69K | Scaled with downloads |
| Uboat Attack (Voodoo) | ~$35K/week | 500K active users, stable |

**Takeaway:** Even niche hyper-casual games clear **$35K-$69K/week** = $150K-$300K/month

## Revenue Models

### 1. Ad-Based (Hyper-Casual)
- Rewarded video eCPM: $15-25 (iOS higher)
- Interstitials: Lower but more volume
- Need millions of sessions/month

### 2. IAP (In-App Purchases)
- Works for games with progression/meta
- Whales drive 80%+ of revenue
- Battle passes, cosmetics, boosters

### 3. Hybrid (Best of Both)
- Ads for casual players
- IAP for engaged players
- Subscriptions for power users

---

## Genres That Print Money

### 1. Match-3 / Puzzle
- Royal Match, Candy Crush model
- Long sessions, high retention
- IAP + ads hybrid
- **Verdict:** Competitive but proven

### 2. Idle/Clicker Games
- Low dev cost, high engagement
- "Plays itself" = low churn
- Ad-heavy monetization
- **Verdict:** Good for solo devs

### 3. Hybrid-Casual
- Simple core + meta layers
- Best of hyper-casual (easy) + mid-core (retention)
- **Verdict:** Rising star, less saturated

### 4. Simulation/Tycoon
- Business sim, farming, etc.
- High engagement, IAP potential
- **Verdict:** More dev time but higher LTV

## No-Code Game Builders

### Platforms

**1. GDevelop (Free/Open Source)**
- Visual scripting, no coding needed
- Built-in monetization via gd.games hosting
- **Earn automatically:** Host on gd.games → paid per session
- Companies making >$50K/year must use Pro plan (proof people make money)
- Best for: Arcade-style, 2D games, open-source flexibility

**2. Buildbox (Paid)**
- Drag-and-drop, zero code
- "Multiple revenue-generating games launched every month"
- Built-in ads, IAP, store solutions
- Huge asset library (characters, sounds, backgrounds)
- Best for: Hyper-casual mobile games, rapid releases

**3. Construct 3**
- Browser-based, visual scripting
- Cross-platform exports
- Monetization integrations
- Best for: Hobbyists and indie devs

## The No-Code Game Play

**Strategy:** Rapid-release studios use Buildbox to pump out hyper-casual games monthly without programmers.

**Economics:**
- Build time: 1-4 weeks per game (no coding)
- Cost: Buildbox subscription (~$20-100/mo) + art assets
- Revenue: Ads + IAP (see hyper-casual numbers above)
- Volume game: Launch 5-10 games, 1-2 hit = profit

**Risk:** Saturated market, need marketing/ASO skills. But faster to test than coding from scratch.
