## Day 1: Channel Setup + Niche Decision

### Morning (1 hour): YouTube Channel Setup

1. **Create your channel**
   - Go to YouTube → click your profile → Create a channel
   - Name: Pick something evergreen and niche-appropriate. Examples:
     - Finance: "Wealth Decoded" / "Money Plain and Simple"
     - History: "Hidden History" / "The Dark Files"
     - Prediction Markets: "Market Oracle" / "The Prediction Lab"
   - Don't overthink this. You can change it later.

2. **Channel Art**
   - Go to Canva → YouTube Channel Art template
   - Use a simple design: channel name + tagline + brand color
   - Download → upload to YouTube
   - **Time: 15 minutes max.** Move on.

3. **Profile Picture**
   - Use a logo (not a face)
   - Canva has free logo templates. Or use a simple color + first letter.

4. **Channel Description**
   - Write 2–3 sentences about what your channel covers
   - Include your main keyword in the description naturally

5. **Set your channel to "Not for Kids"** (unless it literally is for kids)

---

### Afternoon (1 hour): Pick Your Niche + First Topic

**Step 1: Decide on a niche (15 minutes)**

If you already know (prediction markets, finance, etc.) — done.

If unsure, pick from this shortlist based on your interests:
- Like numbers/data → **Finance or Prediction Markets**
- Like history → **Dark History or True Crime**
- Like tech → **AI Tools Explained**
- Like philosophy → **Stoicism/Mindset**

**You must pick one today.** You cannot research your way to perfect. Ship something.

---

**Step 2: Find your first topic (30 minutes)**

**Method 1 — YouTube search hack:**
1. Go to YouTube
2. Type your niche keyword + space in the search bar
3. Look at the autocomplete suggestions → these are what people are searching
4. Pick one that has: (a) autocomplete appearing = search demand, (b) top videos have <500K views = not dominated, (c) you can explain in 5–10 minutes

**Method 2 — Steal from trending:**
1. Sort YouTube by "This week" in your niche
2. Find a video with 50K+ views, <14 days old
3. Your angle: Cover the same topic but from a different angle, or go deeper

**Method 3 — Ask Claude (free at claude.ai):**
```
I want to start a faceless YouTube channel about [your niche].
Give me 10 video title ideas that are currently trending, easy to 
explain without a face, and would work well with AI-generated visuals.
Format: Title | Why it would work | What the hook should be
```

**Your first video topic should be:**
- A question people Google/search
- Explainable in 5–7 minutes
- Something you understand well enough to fact-check the script

**Write your chosen topic in a notepad. You're done with Day 1.**

## Day 2: Script + Voiceover

### Generate Your Script (45 minutes)

**Go to claude.ai (free tier) and use this exact prompt:**

```
You are a professional YouTube scriptwriter for a [YOUR NICHE] channel.
Write a YouTube video script for: "[YOUR VIDEO TITLE]"

Requirements:
- Target length: 700–900 words (5 minutes when read aloud)
- Conversational tone — write for someone to SPEAK, not read
- Short sentences. No jargon. No "In conclusion."
- Start with a strong hook: a question or shocking fact
- Include [PAUSE] markers every 3–4 sentences
- Include [VISUAL: brief scene description] every 100 words
- End with CTA: "Subscribe for more videos like this"
- DO NOT include intro music, credits, or titles

Format:
[HOOK - first 30 seconds]
[SECTION 1]
[SECTION 2]
[SECTION 3]
[OUTRO]
```

**Read through the script once.** Fix anything that sounds unnatural when read aloud. Tweak maybe 5–10% of it. Trust the AI — this is practice, not perfection.

**Save the script** as `video1_script.txt`

Also save a clean version (no [VISUAL] or [PAUSE] markers) as `video1_voiceover.txt`. You'll use this for TTS.

**Estimated time: 45 minutes** (10 to generate + 35 to review + minor edits)

---

### Generate Voiceover (30 minutes)

**Go to ElevenLabs (elevenlabs.io)**

1. Create free account
2. Go to Text to Speech
3. **Choose a voice:** Browse the library. Pick one that fits your channel's tone:
   - Finance: "Adam" (authoritative) or "Daniel" (calm)
   - History: "Thomas" (dramatic) or "Liam" (gravelly)
   - Motivation: "Josh" (energetic) or "Nicole" (warm)
   - **Tip:** Preview each voice with your first sentence. Pick the one that sounds like your channel.
4. Paste your clean voiceover script
5. Settings: Stability 50%, Similarity 75%, Style 40%
6. Generate → Download MP3

**Free tier gives you 10,000 characters.** A 900-word script ≈ 5,000 characters. You have room for 2 test generates.

**Save as** `video1_voiceover.mp3`

**Listen to the full audio.** If a sentence sounds robotic or wrong, fix just that sentence and re-generate that section. ElevenLabs lets you regenerate individual paragraphs.

## Day 3: Visuals + Assembly

This is the most time-consuming day. Budget 3 hours.

### Find Your Video Clips (1 hour)

**Pexels (pexels.com) — free HD stock footage**

1. Open your script with the [VISUAL] cues
2. For each visual cue, go to Pexels and search for related footage
3. Download the best matching clip (HD quality, landscape orientation)
4. Aim for 1 clip per 10–15 seconds of voiceover

**Example visual cues → Pexels search terms:**
- "aerial view of city" → search "city aerial"
- "stock market going up" → search "stock market charts"
- "person stressed about money" → search "stressed woman finance"
- "ancient Rome" → search "roman columns architecture"

**Create a folder** `video1_clips/` and save all clips there.

**For a 5-minute video:** You need ~20–25 clips. Download 30 to have choices.

**Alternative free sources:**
- Pixabay.com (also free, good variety)
- Coverr.co (free, high quality)
- Mixkit.co (free with attribution)

---

### Assemble the Video (1.5 hours)

**Use CapCut Web (capcut.com) — free, no download required**

1. Create new project → 16:9 (YouTube) or 9:16 (TikTok/Shorts)
2. **Upload your voiceover MP3** → drag onto timeline as audio track
3. **Upload all your video clips** → add them to the video track in order
4. **Match clips to voiceover:** 
   - Listen to the voiceover while watching the timeline
   - Cut clips to match what's being talked about
   - A clip should change roughly every 5–10 seconds
5. **Add background music:**
   - CapCut has a built-in music library (royalty-free)
   - Volume: voiceover at 100%, music at 10–15%
6. **Basic color grading:** Use CapCut's auto-enhance or a simple "Cinematic" preset

**Don't spend more than 1.5 hours here.** First video doesn't need to be perfect. It needs to exist.

**Export:** 1080p, H.264, max quality → `video1_raw.mp4`

## Day 4: Captions + Thumbnail + Final

### Add Captions (45 minutes)

**Option A: CapCut Auto-Captions (easiest)**
1. In CapCut → Captions → Auto Captions
2. Select language → Generate
3. Review captions for errors (fix any that are wrong)
4. Style: Large bold text, centered, white with black outline
5. Export with captions burned in

**Option B: Whisper API (better accuracy, requires API key)**
- If you have OpenAI API credits:
  - Upload your MP3 to Whisper API (`response_format: srt`)
  - Import the SRT into CapCut: Captions → Import Captions
  - Style as above

**Export final video:** `video1_final.mp4`

---

### Create Thumbnail (30 minutes)

**Use Canva (canva.com)**

The thumbnail formula that gets clicks:
- **Background:** Relevant image (from your Pexels downloads or a dramatic AI-generated image from Google Imagen, free)
- **Text:** 3–5 words max, HUGE font, high contrast
- **Color scheme:** Pick 2 colors that match your channel brand and use them consistently
- **Emotion:** Thumbnail should convey curiosity, shock, or desire

**Step by step:**
1. In Canva → Create design → YouTube Thumbnail (1280 × 720)
2. Add your background image (use one of your Pexels clips' frames)
3. Add title text — bold, large, contrasting color
4. Use Canva's "Text Effects" → add a drop shadow or outline for readability
5. Download as JPG

**Examples by niche:**
- Finance: "$0 to $10,000 in 3 Months" on a stock chart background
- History: "THE SECRET THEY HID FOR 500 YEARS" over a dark historical image
- Prediction: "This Market Is WRONG" over Polymarket screenshot

## Day 5: Upload + Optimize

### Upload to YouTube (30 minutes)

1. Go to YouTube Studio → Create → Upload Video
2. Upload `video1_final.mp4`
3. **Title:** Your full video title (include your main keyword)
4. **Description template:**
```
[First 2 sentences: what this video is about — include keywords]

In this video, you'll learn:
• [Key point 1]
• [Key point 2]
• [Key point 3]

⏱ Timestamps:
0:00 Intro
0:45 [Section 1]
2:30 [Section 2]
4:00 [Section 3]

🔔 Subscribe for more [NICHE] content every week.

---
[Any affiliate links or relevant links here]
```

5. **Tags:** 10–15 tags. Include: main keyword, niche keywords, and long-tail phrases people might search.

6. **Thumbnail:** Upload your Canva thumbnail JPG

7. **Category:** Choose the most relevant category

8. **Subtitles:** Upload your SRT file if you have one, OR let YouTube auto-generate

9. **Visibility:** 
   - If you're confident: Publish Now
   - If not ready: Schedule for Tuesday–Thursday, 12pm–3pm your target audience's timezone

### After Upload: Optimize Chapters
- Go back to the description and add timestamp links
- This gets your video featured in "Key Moments" in search

## Day 6–7: Analyze + Plan Week 2

### Review Performance (Day 6)

After 24–48 hours, check YouTube Studio analytics:
- **Click-through rate (CTR):** Aim for >4% long-term. First video might be 1–3%.
- **Average view duration:** Aim for >40% of video length.
- **Impressions:** How many times YouTube showed your thumbnail.

**What to learn from your first video:**
- What's the drop-off point? (Where people stop watching) → That section was too long or boring
- Did the thumbnail get clicks? → If CTR is under 3%, the thumbnail needs work
- Did people watch to the end? → If yes, your format is working

### Plan Week 2 (Day 7)

Generate 5 more topic ideas using the same Claude prompt as Day 2, but this time:
- Use insights from your first video
- If people dropped off at 3 minutes → make shorter videos (3–4 min)
- If people watched to the end → same format again

**Build a Topic Queue:**
Make a simple Google Sheet with columns:
- Topic | Status | Publish Date | Views (after 30 days) | Notes

Add 4–5 topics for the next 2 weeks. This is your production queue.

## Common First-Video Mistakes (And How to Avoid Them)

| Mistake | Why it happens | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Spending 3 days perfecting the script | Perfectionism | Set a 45-minute limit. Done > perfect. |
| Not publishing because "it's not good enough" | Fear | Your first video should be bad. That's normal. |
| Using the wrong aspect ratio | Confusion | YouTube: 16:9 (1920×1080). TikTok: 9:16 (1080×1920). |
| Forgetting to add chapters in description | Oversight | Do this immediately after uploading |
| Using copyrighted music | Ignorance | Only use Pexels music, CapCut library, or YouTube Audio Library |
| Title too vague | No keyword research | Include the phrase people would actually search |
| Giving up after 0 views in first 48 hours | Impatience | Normal. YouTube takes 3–7 days to index new videos properly. |
