# Motion Elevation — Sales Call Script
## For Lyubo | English | Conversational, Not Corporate

> **Mindset before the call:** You're not selling — you're diagnosing. Your job is to ask good questions, actually listen, and then show them something useful. If it's a fit, the close happens naturally.

---

## PRE-CALL CHECKLIST (2 min before)

- [ ] Know their business name and what they do
- [ ] Know which sequence they came from (local service / e-com / solopreneur)
- [ ] Have your n8n demo workflow ready to screenshare
- [ ] Quiet environment, headphones in
- [ ] Notepad open — you'll want to write down their exact pain words

---

## OPENING — 30-Second Pitch
*They just joined. First 30 seconds set the tone. Be human, not a robot.*

**Say:**
> "Hey [Name], thanks for jumping on! I'll keep this short — I know your time is valuable.
>
> Super quick on who I am: I'm Lyubo, I run Motion Elevation. We build AI automation workflows for businesses in the DACH market — basically, we take the stuff that's eating your time and automate it in 48 hours.
>
> But honestly? I don't want to pitch you anything today. I just want to understand what's going on in your business and see if there's something I can actually help with. Sound good?"

*Then stop talking. Let them respond. This sets a collaborative tone immediately.*

---

## DISCOVERY — 5 Questions That Reveal the Pain
*This is the most important part of the call. Go slow. Let them vent. The more they talk, the better you understand where the real pain is.*

**Question 1 — Understand their day:**
> "Walk me through a typical week for you — what are the tasks that just keep showing up on your to-do list but feel like they should be handled somehow automatically by now?"

*(Listen for: repetitive tasks, manual follow-ups, things they hate doing)*

---

**Question 2 — Find the bottleneck:**
> "If I could take one thing off your plate starting tomorrow — what would it be?"

*(This is gold. Whatever they say first is the thing they want solved most. Write it down word for word.)*

---

**Question 3 — Quantify the pain:**
> "How much time do you think you're spending on [the thing they mentioned] per week? Like roughly — even a ballpark."

*(They'll usually underestimate. That's fine. Any number gives you something to work with.)*

---

**Question 4 — Reveal the cost:**
> "And what does an hour of your time actually cost you? Not what you charge clients — what could you be doing with that time instead?"

*(This isn't about money math — it's about making them feel the opportunity cost. Let them answer.)*

---

**Question 5 — Uncover urgency:**
> "Has this been a problem for a while, or is it something that's getting worse as you grow?"

*(If it's getting worse = urgency. If it's always been there = you need to create urgency.)*

---

## TRANSITION TO PITCH
*Summarize what you heard back to them. This is called mirroring — it makes them feel understood and makes your pitch land better.*

**Say:**
> "Okay, so if I'm hearing you right — [X] is eating a lot of your time, it's been like this for a while, and the real cost isn't just the time itself but the fact that you're not focusing on [Y]. Is that about right?"

*(They'll say yes, or correct you — both are fine. Then:)*

> "Cool. Let me show you something I built that I think is pretty directly relevant to what you just described."

---

## DEMO MOMENT — What to Show on Screen

*Share your screen. Open your n8n canvas. Walk them through a real workflow — don't just show slides.*

**The 3-part demo structure:**

**1. The Trigger (30 sec)**
> "So this is n8n — it's the automation platform we build on. Think of it like a flowchart that runs 24/7. Right here is the trigger — in your case, this would be [new booking / new lead / new order / form submission]. The second that happens, the whole flow kicks off."

**2. The Magic (60 sec)**
> "From there, a few things happen automatically. First, [relevant action #1 — e.g., confirmation email sent]. Then [relevant action #2 — e.g., reminder SMS 24h before]. Then [relevant action #3 — e.g., if they don't confirm, they get a rebooking link]. All of this happens without you touching anything."

**3. The Outcome (30 sec)**
> "The business owners I've set this up for usually tell me the same thing: they stop worrying about [the pain point]. It just... handles itself. That's the whole idea."

*Then go quiet. Let it land. Don't rush to speak.*

---

## PRICING PRESENTATION

*Don't lead with price. Only present after the demo, when they're already seeing the value.*

**Say:**
> "So there are three ways we work together, depending on what makes sense for your situation."

| Tier | Price | What's Included |
|------|-------|-----------------|
| **Starter** | €297/mo | 1 automation workflow, setup + maintenance, monthly check-in |
| **Growth** | €697/mo | 3 automation workflows, priority support, monthly optimization |
| **Scale** | €1,497/mo | Full automation stack, dedicated support, custom integrations |

**Then say:**
> "Most people I talk to start with Starter or Growth. It really depends on how many processes you want to tackle first. What feels like the right starting point based on what we talked about?"

*Throw the question back. Let them choose, don't push.*

---

## HANDLING OBJECTIONS

### Objection 1: "That's too expensive."

**Don't argue. Reframe:**
> "I get that. Let me ask you something — how much time are you spending on [the pain point] per week? [Wait for answer]. And what's that worth to you?
>
> If this saves you even 4–5 hours a week, and you're valuing your time at €50–100/hour... that's €800–2,000/month in recovered capacity. The automation pays for itself pretty fast.
>
> But I hear you — if budget is tight right now, we can start with just one workflow and see how it goes. The Starter plan is €297. Want to go from there?"

---

### Objection 2: "I need to think about it."

**Don't panic. Ask what's actually going on:**
> "Of course, totally understand. Can I ask — is there something specific you're unsure about, or is it more of a timing thing? I just want to make sure I haven't left any questions unanswered."

*(Usually there's a real objection hiding here — price, trust, uncertainty. Surface it and address it directly.)*

> "If it's a trust thing — which is totally valid, you just met me — I'm happy to do a small paid pilot first. We pick one workflow, build it, you see it work. Then you decide if you want to keep going. No long-term commitment."

---

### Objection 3: "I could do this myself."

**Validate, then reframe:**
> "You probably could, honestly. n8n isn't that complicated once you get into it. The question is — do you want to? Learning it, setting it up, debugging it when something breaks... that's 20–40 hours of your time minimum to get one workflow running properly.
>
> I've already done that learning. You're paying me to skip it. If your time is worth more than the monthly fee, it makes sense. If you genuinely want to learn it yourself, I'll even point you to the right resources — no hard feelings."

---

## THE CLOSE

*Don't be weird about this. If the conversation went well, close naturally.*

**If they're clearly interested:**
> "Sounds like the Growth plan makes the most sense for where you're at. Want to get started? I can send you the agreement and invoice today, and we can kick off the first workflow this week."

**If they're on the fence:**
> "Here's what I'd suggest — let's start with one workflow, just to prove the concept. If it works and saves you time, we expand. If not, no hard feelings. Does that feel fair?"

**If they need more time:**
> "No problem at all. I'll send you a quick summary of what we talked about and what I'd build for you — just so you have something concrete to look at. Then you decide. Sound good?"

---

## NEXT STEPS

Always leave the call with a clear next action. One of these three:

1. **They said yes** → Send agreement + invoice within 2 hours. Set kickoff call date.
2. **They said maybe** → Send follow-up email with personalized proposal within 24 hours.
3. **They said no** → Thank them, ask if you can check back in 3 months, wish them well.

**Follow-up email template (post-call):**

> Hey [Name],
>
> Great talking today. Here's a quick summary of what I'd build for you:
>
> **Workflow:** [e.g., Appointment reminder + no-show recovery]
> **What it does:** [2-3 sentences, their words not yours]
> **Timeline:** 48 hours from kickoff
> **Price:** €[X]/month
>
> Here's how to get started: [link to agreement or calendar]
>
> Let me know if you have any questions.
>
> Lyubo

---

## QUICK TIPS FOR THE CALL

- **Silence is okay.** Don't fill every pause. Let them think.
- **Use their exact words** when you pitch back to them. It's weirdly powerful.
- **Don't oversell.** If it's not a fit, say so. Builds way more trust than pushing.
- **Be yourself.** You're 20-something building something real — that's actually a strength. People root for founders.
- **Energy matters.** If you're excited about what you built, it shows. If you're nervous, that shows too. Be calm, curious, helpful.
