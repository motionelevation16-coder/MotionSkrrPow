# Cold Email Templates That Work

*Tested frameworks. Copy, personalize, send.*

---

## Framework 1: The Pain Question

Opens with a question that makes them think about their problem.

```
Subject: Quick question about {Company}

Hi {Name},

How many hours does your team spend on {repetitive task} each week?

For most {industry} businesses I talk to, it's 15-20+ hours.

We built automations that cut that to near zero — AI handles the 
{task examples}, you just review the results.

Most clients save $3-4k/month in labor costs.

Worth a 15-min call to see if it fits?

{Your calendar link}

Best,
{Your name}
```

### Personalization Points:
- {repetitive task} = something specific to their industry
- {industry} = their exact business type
- {task examples} = 2-3 concrete examples

## Framework 2: The Observation

Shows you did research. Creates curiosity.

```
Subject: Noticed something on {Company} website

Hi {Name},

Was looking at {Company} and noticed you're {observation}.

That usually means {pain assumption}.

We help {industry} businesses automate {specific thing} — typically 
saves 10-15 hours/week.

Just helped {similar company type} go from {before} to {after}.

Curious if this is something you've thought about?

{Calendar link} if worth a quick chat.

{Your name}
```

### Examples of Observations:
- "noticed you're posting manually to 4 platforms"
- "noticed your contact page goes to a general inbox"
- "noticed you're still scheduling via email back-and-forth"
- "noticed your team is hiring for {role that could be automated}"

---

## Framework 3: The Social Proof

Leads with a relevant result.

```
Subject: How {Similar Company} saved {X} hours/week

Hi {Name},

Just helped a {industry} business like {Company} automate their 
{process}.

Result: {specific metric}

Here's what changed:
- Before: {manual process}
- After: {automated version}
- Time saved: {hours}/week

Would this be useful for {Company}?

Happy to share exactly how it works: {calendar link}

{Your name}
```

### Metrics That Work:
- "Saved 15 hours/week on email"
- "Went from 3 hours/day posting to 20 minutes"
- "Cut response time from 24 hours to 5 minutes"
- "Increased follow-up rate from 30% to 100%"

## Framework 4: The Breakup

Last email in sequence. Often gets highest replies.

```
Subject: Should I close your file?

Hi {Name},

I've reached out a couple times and haven't heard back — 
totally get it, you're busy.

Just want to check: should I close your file?

If automating {their pain} isn't a priority right now, no worries 
at all. I won't bother you again.

But if you've been meaning to reply and just haven't gotten 
around to it — here's my calendar: {link}

Either way, all good.

{Your name}
```

### Why It Works:
- Removes pressure
- Gives them permission to say no
- Creates closure urgency
- "Close your file" triggers loss aversion

---

## Framework 5: The Value Bomb

Leads with something useful, asks for nothing.

```
Subject: Quick resource for {their challenge}

Hi {Name},

Put together a guide on {topic relevant to them} — thought you 
might find it useful given what you're building at {Company}.

{Link to resource}

No strings attached. Just genuinely think it could help.

If you ever want to chat about automating {their thing}, I'm around.

{Your name}
```

### Resources to Create:
- Simple PDF guide ("5 Ways to Automate X")
- Loom video walkthrough
- Template/spreadsheet
- Checklist

---

## Framework 6: The Referral Ask

When they're not the right person.

```
Subject: Quick favor?

Hi {Name},

I reached out last week about automation for {Company} — 
realize you might not be the right person for this.

Would you mind pointing me to whoever handles {operations/tech/marketing}?

Just want to make sure I'm not wasting your time.

Thanks either way.

{Your name}
```

## Subject Line Templates

### Curiosity:
- "Quick question about {Company}"
- "Noticed something about {Company}"
- "Idea for {Company}"

### Social Proof:
- "How {Similar Company} did {result}"
- "{X} hours saved at {Similar Company}"

### Direct:
- "{Pain point} at {Company}?"
- "Re: {Company} automation"

### Breakup:
- "Should I close your file?"
- "One last thing"
- "Not a fit?"

### Pattern Interrupt:
- "This might not be for you"
- "Weird question"
- "Don't read this if you love manual work"

## Follow-Up Sequence

### Day 0: Initial email (Framework 1 or 2)
### Day 2: Short bump
```
Subject: Re: [original subject]

Hi {Name},

Just floating this back up. Did you get a chance to look?

{Your name}
```

### Day 5: Value add (Framework 5)
```
Subject: Re: [original subject]

Hi {Name},

In case it's helpful — here's {resource} we put together on {topic}.

{Link}

Let me know if you'd like to chat about automating this for {Company}.

{Your name}
```

### Day 8: Breakup (Framework 4)
```
Subject: Should I close your file?

[Breakup template]
```

## A/B Test Ideas

### Subject Lines:
- Question vs Statement
- With company name vs Without
- Specific pain vs General benefit

### Openings:
- Question lead vs Observation lead
- Direct vs Soft approach

### CTAs:
- Calendar link vs "Want to chat?"
- Specific time offer vs Open-ended
- "Worth 15 mins?" vs "Happy to show you how"

## Response Templates

### When They Show Interest:
```
Great to hear from you, {Name}!

Here's my calendar — pick any time that works: {link}

Looking forward to it.

{Your name}
```

### When They Ask for More Info:
```
Happy to share more!

In short: we build automations that handle {their pain}. 
Most clients save {metric}.

Here's a quick example: {link to case study or loom}

Worth a 15-min call to see if it fits your setup?

{Calendar link}
```

### When They Say Not Right Now:
```
Totally understand — timing matters.

Mind if I check back in {timeframe}? Or feel free to reach out 
when it makes sense.

No pressure either way.

{Your name}
```

### When They Say No Thanks:
```
Appreciate you letting me know, {Name}. 

If things change down the road, I'm here.

Best of luck with {Company}!

{Your name}
```

## Tracking What Works

For each template, track:
- **Send count**
- **Open rate** (if using email tool)
- **Reply rate**
- **Positive reply rate**
- **Calls booked**

Double down on what works. Kill what doesn't.
